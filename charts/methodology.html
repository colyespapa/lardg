Methodology Guide

Lardish Charts - Data-Driven Music Curation

Overview

Lardish Charts operates on a "Deep Data" model. To ensure the highest accuracy in tracking trends, peaks, and re-entries, the system monitors a significantly larger pool of data than what is displayed on the public interface. This document outlines the internal mechanics of our weekly and monthly rankings.

1. Weekly Chart Dynamics

The "Top 100" Backend

While the website displays a streamlined Top 20, the internal database tracks the full Top 100 positions.

  • Invisible Tracking: Any song ranking between #21 and #100 is fully logged. This allows the system to differentiate between a brand-new entry and a song that has been climbing through the lower tiers.
  • Status Accuracy: If a song moves from #25 to #15, the system correctly identifies it as a "climber" rather than a "NEW" entry.
  • Historical Persistence: We maintain the "Peak Position" across the entire Top 100, ensuring that a song's true highest point is never lost, even if it occurred outside the visible Top 20.

2. Monthly Chart Consolidation

Artist & Song Aggregation

Monthly charts are designed to highlight the most consistent performers. To achieve this, we process the Top 25 entries to generate a curated Top 10 showcase.

Category Data Processed (Internal) Data Displayed (Public)
Weekly Songs Top 100 Top 20
Monthly Songs Top 25 Top 10
Monthly Artists Top 25 Top 10

By monitoring 25 artists but only showing 10, the "Change" indicator remains mathematically sound. If an artist drops to #14, they disappear from the view but remain "active" in the system, allowing for accurate "RE-ENTRY" or "OUT" status detection in subsequent months.

3. Status Indicators & Logic

  • NEW: The very first time a title/artist enters the internal tracking system (Top 100 for weeks / Top 25 for months).
  • RE-ENTRY: A return to the chart after falling completely out of the internal tracking range in previous updates.
  • OUT: Automatic status assigned when a title is no longer present in the most recent update's internal tracking range.
  • PEAK: The absolute highest rank achieved since the song's debut in the system.
